# Escalations & Internal Notes

Clear, concise escalations and internal notes help other teams understand the issue quickly and prevent unnecessary back-and-forth. These templates focus on summarising accurately, reducing noise, and providing the exact details another agent or technical team needs.

---

## 1. Internal Notes – Summarising a Case Clearly

> **Summary:** Customer unable to {issue}.  
> **Attempted steps:** {brief steps tried}.  
> **Outcome:** Issue persists / partially improved / not reproducible.  
> **Customer impact:** {e.g., blocked from using feature / payment failed / time-sensitive}.  
> **Next steps:** Awaiting engineering input / require approval / monitoring.  
> **Attachments:** Screenshots/logs included.

Short, structured, and easy for the next agent to scan.

---

## 2. Escalation to Technical Team – Reproducible Issue

> **Issue:** Confirmed reproducible bug affecting {feature}.  
> **Steps to reproduce:**  
> 1. {Step 1}  
> 2. {Step 2}  
> 3. {Step 3}  
> **Expected result:** {what should happen}.  
> **Actual result:** {what happens instead}.  
>
> **Customer details:**  
> - Account ID: {ID}  
> - Device/Browser/App Version: {details}  
> - Region/Time zone: {details}  
>
> **Impact level:** {High / Medium / Low}  
>
> **Notes:** Customer waiting for an update; issue blocking {action}.  
>
> **Attachments:** Screenshots/logs included.

This gives engineers exactly what they need.

---

## 3. Escalation to Technical Team – Not Reproducible Yet

> **Issue:** Customer reports {problem}, but not reproducible internally.  
>
> **Customer environment:**  
> - Device: {device}  
> - OS/version: {version}  
> - App/browser: {details}  
>
> **Customer steps:** {steps they took}.  
> **Internal steps:** Tried {steps}, unable to reproduce.  
>
> **Request:**  
> - Guidance on potential causes  
> - Any known issues affecting {feature}  
> - Additional debug steps for customer  
>
> **Attachments:** Customer screenshot/log included.

Shows you're thorough without pretending to know more than you do.

---

## 4. Escalation to Billing/Finance Team

> **Issue:** Customer disputing charge for {amount} on {date}.  
>
> **Findings:**  
> - Charge valid/unclear/duplicated (select one)  
> - Policy relevant: {policy details}  
> - Customer reasoning: {what they said}  
>
> **Request:**  
> - Review eligibility for refund/credit  
> - Confirm correct charge  
> - Clarify discrepancy  
>
> **Notes:** Customer requesting update within {timeframe}.  
>
> **Attachments:** Invoice/account history attached.

Billing teams LOVE clean escalations like this.

---

## 5. Escalation to Supervisor – Behaviour/Tone Sensitive

> **Context:** Customer upset due to {reason}.  
> **Tone:** escalated / aggressive / distressed (select one).  
> **Attempts:** Provided {explanations/solutions}, customer remains dissatisfied.  
>
> **Request:** Supervisor review and possible intervention.  
>
> **Notes:** Customer expects a callback/email within {timeframe}.

Shows maturity and safety awareness.

---

## 6. Escalation – Request for Manual Adjustment (Credit, Refund, etc.)

> **Request:** Approve {refund/credit/adjustment} of **{amount}** for {reason}.  
>
> **Findings:**  
> - Within/Outside policy  
> - Customer history: {brief notes if relevant}  
> - Justification: {clear reason}  
>
> **Risk level:** Low / Medium / High  
> **Impact:** Prevents churn / corrects error / goodwill gesture  
>
> **Attachments:** Screenshot of billing issue attached.

Perfect for showing good judgement.

---

## 7. Template for Clean Case Handover (Shift Change)

> **Customer:** {name} – {issue}  
> **Status:** Waiting on {team/response}.  
> **Actions taken:** {steps completed}.  
> **Next steps:** Follow up by {timeframe}.  
> **Notes:** Customer expects {callback/update}.  
>
> **Priority:** {Low/Medium/High}.  
> **Attachments:** All added.

This is the exact kind of structure Support Ops teams beg for.
